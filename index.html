PI: Yi Zuo, Ph.D.

This work investigated how early-life penicillin exposure affects the processing of sensory information both on the behavioral and the neurobiological level. On the behavioral level, it identified abnormalities in sensory as well as sensory-motor gating behaviors. On the neurobiological level, it identified changes in the development of inhibitory circuits as well as the morphology and process dynamics of microglia in the sensory processing region of the cerebral cortex. This work also explored how perturbations to the community structure of gut microbiota and changes in inflammatory signaling might mediate the identified behavioral and neurobiological abnormalities.
